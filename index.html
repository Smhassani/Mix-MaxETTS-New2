Mix-MaxETTS: a text-to-emotional speech synthesis model based on deep encoder-decoder structure for transfer secondary emotions

Seyyed Mahdi Hassani, Mohammad Reza Kangavari

Abstract

Given the noteworthy importance of emotions in social interaction, emotional speech synthesis has attracted significant attention from human-computer interaction. Remarkable advancements have been made in emotional text-to-speech synthesis to effectively convey emotions in generated speech. Most previous studies have concentrated on imitating styles associated with a specific primary emotion, neglecting secondary emotions that arise from mixtures of primary emotions. However, there is a need to leverage primary and secondary emotions in speech synthesis to facilitate more engaging, realistic, and natural interactions in social artificial agents. To address this gap, we propose a text-to-emotional speech synthesizing model designed to generate nuanced mixtures of emotions, effectively conveying secondary emotions during interactions. By adjusting the values of each basic emotion, we can control the mix of emotions in synthetic speech. Specifically, the proposed method is a sequence-to-sequence model that comprises four main components:(1) a linguistic encoder that transforms text into linguistic embedding, capturing semantic meanings, (2) a pre-trained multi-speaker verification model that obtains speaker identity embedding, ensuring that the synthesized speech reflects the desired speaker characteristics, (3) an emotion-aware encoder that learns both speaker-independent mixed emotion style and speaker-dependent emotion speaker style representations, capturing the complexities of varying emotional expressions in speech, and (4) a decoder to synthesize mixed emotional speech based on the resultant embeddings. Our proposed method distinguishes between primary emotions and the variations in mixed emotions while learning about emotional styles. We achieve emotional mixture prosody variations by employing a pre-train speaker independent speech emotion recognition model. The effectiveness of our proposed framework has been validated through objective and subjective evaluations.
